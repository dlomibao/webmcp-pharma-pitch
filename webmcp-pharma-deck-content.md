# WebMCP for Pharma Brands
### A pitch deck built around a live demo · For agency account leads presenting to pharma client execs

---

## The framing

This entire pitch is anchored on one artifact — an interactive demo of a fictional cancer drug, **Veritrax®** (veritraxib), built specifically to show how a WebMCP-enabled pharma site behaves in the AI era.

🔗 **Demo link:** https://claude.ai/public/artifacts/b5f575d2-4891-4bb9-97f0-cdf307609f20

**Why we built a fictional drug.** Every statistic, brand logo, or real-product reference creates legal, regulatory, and competitive complications. Veritrax is a made-up CDK4/6 inhibitor for HR+/HER2− metastatic breast cancer. It does not exist. The dosing, interactions, and side-effect data are illustrative. This lets clients engage with the *mechanism* — how WebMCP changes the brand/AI relationship — without getting tangled in "is that claim on-label for our product." Once the mechanism lands, you can say *"now imagine this on [Client Brand]."*

**The deck's job is to set up the demo and land the implications.** Everything before slide 8 exists to make the client hungry to see it. Everything after exists to convert that "aha" into a pilot.

## How to use this document

Each slide below includes:

- **Title** — what appears on screen
- **Headline** — the sentence the slide lives or dies by
- **Body** — supporting copy / bullets / stats (lean; cut before adding)
- **Talk track** — what the account lead says out loud
- **Visual note** — what's actually on the slide
- **Demo callback** — how the slide connects forward/back to the live demo

Target: 30-minute delivery + 15-minute discussion.

## SLIDE 7 — The new layer has a name

**Headline:** **WebMCP. Launched February 2026 by Google and Microsoft. Already shipping in Chrome.**

**Body:**
- Co-developed by Google and Microsoft through the W3C
- Shipped in Chrome 146 as of March 2026
- A browser-native way for a website to publish a **menu of tools** any AI agent can call directly
- Think of it as **schema markup for the AI era** — but 10× more powerful

**It is NOT:**
- A chatbot
- An AI product you buy
- A new website you build
- A replacement for your brand site

**It IS:**
- A set of instructions your website publishes so every AI — today and in the future — reads the right answer, from the right place, every time

**Talk track:**
*"WebMCP stands for Web Model Context Protocol. Don't memorize it. Here's what matters: Google and Microsoft co-wrote it, it's a new web standard, and it went live in Chrome in March. In plain English, your website posts a menu. On the menu are items like 'dosing information,' 'check interactions,' 'list side effects.' When an AI agent visits your site — on behalf of a patient, a prescriber, or because Google is building an AI Overview — it reads the menu and picks the right item. The AI doesn't generate the answer. Your website serves it, pre-approved, exactly as you wrote it. Which brings me to the part your MLR team needs to hear."*

**Visual note:** One graphic: a registry of tool cards on the left, multiple AI logos on the right, clean line connections.

**Demo callback:** *"Which brings me to the part your MLR team needs to hear"* — teeing up slide 8.

**Sources:** W3C Web Machine Learning Community Group spec; Chrome 146 release notes; Google/Microsoft joint specification February 10, 2026

## SLIDE 8 — Why this inverts the MLR problem

**Headline:** **The AI reads your answer. It doesn't write it.**

**Body:**

| **Traditional generative AI risk** | **WebMCP-served responses** |
|---|---|
| Non-deterministic (different answer each time) | **Deterministic** — same query, same answer, every time |
| Hallucinates claims, dosages, off-label suggestions | **Pre-reviewed** — every response MLR-locked before exposure |
| No audit trail for regulators | **Auditable** — every call logged, every source is your site |
| Reviewer time spent correcting model drift | **On-brand** — the AI cannot paraphrase or invent |

**Talk track:**
*"Here's the part where the MLR people in your organization should actually get excited. The reason generative AI scares compliance teams is that it writes new sentences every time you ask it something. WebMCP flips that. The AI is no longer the author — it's just the delivery mechanism. Your MLR team approves the answer once. Every AI that queries it gets that exact answer. Same query, same words, every time. No hallucinations. No off-label drift. An audit trail that maps every AI response back to an approved source on your domain. This is the first time AI and MLR have actually been on the same side of the table. Now let me stop talking and show you."*

**Visual note:** Two-column comparison table. Four rows. Keep it scannable.

**Demo callback:** *"Now let me stop talking and show you"* — the hard pivot into the demo.

## SLIDE 11 — What this looks like as a project

**Headline:** **A 60-day pilot. One brand. Five tools.**

**Body:**

**Phase 1 — Discovery** *(weeks 1–2)*
Map your top patient/HCP questions. Identify which already have MLR-approved answers. Identify which need to be authored.

**Phase 2 — Tool architecture** *(weeks 3–4)*
Design the registry: `get_dosing`, `check_interactions`, `get_side_effects`, `get_prescribing_info`, `get_patient_resources`. Each tool = one pre-approved response surface.

**Phase 3 — MLR lock** *(weeks 5–7)*
Route every response through your existing Veeva PromoMats workflow. Nothing ships until approved. Every update flows through the same path.

**Phase 4 — Deploy + monitor** *(weeks 8+)*
Ship to production. Track: AI citations, tool-call volume per platform, response coverage, MLR cycle time on AI-delivered content.

**What we bring:**
Strategy · creative · MLR partnership · technical build · analytics

**What this is not:**
Not a replatform. Not a new site. Not a ten-million-dollar chatbot. Bolts onto the brand site you already have.

# Cutting guide

**If you have 20 minutes instead of 30:**
- Cut **Slide 2** (fold "they're asking about your drug" into Slide 1 talk track)
- Cut **Slide 12** (end on Slide 11 with the pilot scope — stronger ask)

**If you have 10 minutes (discovery call, not full pitch):**
- Open on Slide 3 (the traffic collapse)
- Jump to Slide 7 (what WebMCP is)
- Tee up + run the demo (Slides 8–9)
- Close on Slide 11 (pilot scope)

**Do not cut the demo.** Everything else in the deck is argument. The demo is evidence. If you're ever forced to cut deeper, cut argument slides — never cut slides 8, 9, or 10.
